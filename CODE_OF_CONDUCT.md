# Code of Conduct

OpenControl should be a practical, respectful place to collaborate on desktop automation, MCP tooling, privacy, and Windows systems work. Everyone participating in this project is expected to help keep discussion focused, welcoming, and useful.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and patient with people of different backgrounds, skill levels, and viewpoints.
- Assume good intent, but take responsibility for the impact of your words and actions.
- Keep feedback specific, constructive, and tied to the work.
- Respect privacy and safety boundaries, especially when discussing desktop control, screenshots, logs, blocklists, or user data.
- Use inclusive language and avoid harassment, insults, personal attacks, or repeated unwanted contact.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not welcome in this project:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or abuse of any kind.
- Sexualized language or imagery where it is not directly relevant to the work.
- Doxing, publishing private information, or pressuring someone to reveal private details.
- Discriminatory comments based on identity, background, ability, age, religion, nationality, race, ethnicity, gender identity, sexual orientation, or similar characteristics.
- Deliberately derailing technical discussion, spamming, or repeatedly ignoring maintainer direction.
- Sharing exploit details or sensitive security information publicly instead of following the security reporting process.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues or pull requests, block participants, or take other reasonable action to protect the project and its contributors.

If you need to report a Code of Conduct concern, email `joshua@lawrence.zip`. Include relevant links, screenshots, or context if you can do so safely. Reports will be handled with discretion, and retaliation against anyone who reports a concern is not tolerated.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, release channels, and other public or private spaces where someone is representing the project.
